Genshin Furina Gameplay Overview

  • Element: Hydro
  • Weapon: Sword
  • Rarity: 5-star

  • Furina is an off-field DPS and Support that utilizes her Skill to do impressive off-field damage and using her Burst to offer the team sizeable buffs, allowing her to be the centerpiece of a wide variety of team compositions. Archons share a pattern of being powerful and centralizing, meaning that they are always worth acquiring and will be relevant and valuable for a long time.

  • Furina is also the only character so far who can change her alignment between Pneuma and Ousia at will, allowing her to adapt to any Fontaine puzzle or boss.

Furina does need some help, however, from a strong healing teammate. One of her main traits is draining the team's HP in order to deal damage and/or apply buffs. In longer fights like Spiral Abyss (save for players who have multiple 5-star units with all their constellations), she will eventually drain her team dry. A character with a powerful, fast, teamwide heal will be essential to keep the Furina engine going.

The Hydro Archon also has the widest variety of energy requirements. You'll have to consider your gearing carefully depending on what team she’s in.



  • In teams where she is the only Hydro character, her ER will need to be around 240.

  • With one other Hydro character, it drops down to 200%.

  • If you use powerful batteries like Raiden or Fischl, this drops much further down to 150% or lower.

If you don't want to keep changing your gear for each team, you can sacrifice some damage or buffing utility for a safe 180-200% Energy Recharge.

Using another Hydro teammate is something you’ll probably want to do anyways with Furina, as she has strong synergies with many HP-scaling Hydro characters.

The Furina Team

On-Field Unit

Building a Furina Team requires Furina to be paired with a powerful healer - one that can heal the whole team at once for a large amount to keep Furina's buffs and damage output going. Beyond that, you pair two other damage-based units into the team (especially other Fontainians).

Your healing and DPS choices will simply depend on what elemental reaction you wish to utilize most. It's generally recommended to include at least one other Hydro character, but this isn't required if you can handle her energy needs.

Truly, you can use almost any popular DPS of any element as long as you pair them with a good reaction teammate, so the number of teammates is too long to list. As long as your healer is strong enough, you can slap most things together and just have it work.
